BioinspiredLLM 2022-2023

A fine-tuned large language model trained on biological and bioinspired materials science literature. The model is finetuned using a LoRA strategy after creating a synthetic Q-A distilled dataset. The model was designed and tested for knowledge retrieval abilities, hypothesis generation, as well as agentic potential in creating new materials.

LLMs to Real World Experiment: Plant Materials 2024-2025

Project based on bridging LLM generated content to real-life scientific experimentation. Using a new strategy called Hierarchical Sampling, we developed an LLM-driven framework that can generate and evaluate hundreds to thousands of hypotheses to guide experimental validation. The approach led to the fabrication of a new plant-based adhesive with tunable morphology and measurable shear strength, demonstrating how AI can translate biological inspiration into real materials design as a scientific co-pilot.

Rehabilitation Sensor 2018

Designed and prototyped a wearable medical brace to support post-surgical rehabilitation by detecting movements that could re-injure the surgical site. The device alerts users through light, vibration, and sound feedback. Led mechanical design in a 5-person team, gaining experience with Arduino, CAD, 3D printing, and physical prototyping. Presented at a showcase of 150+ attendees; awarded 3rd place out of 15+ teams.
